About Me

I was often told I was too curious, but that same curiosity is the foundation of my career.

Hello! I’m a Computer Science (Honours) graduate with a passion for solving problems through data analysis or helping users troubleshoot tech issues. I enjoy exploring how systems work, asking the right questions, and turning complexity into practical, meaningful solutions.

Over the course of my studies, I have built neural networks, classified social media profiles, benchmarked image recognition models, and explored how data can support better business decisions. But I have also worked in roles that required clear communication, empathy, and real-time problem-solving like volunteering as a Technology Assistant or supporting users during large-scale public events.

What really sets me apart is my analytical thinking and people-centered support. I love tackling challenges, finding stories behind numbers, and resolving a technical issue for someone who is stuck. For this mindset, UOW Pulse referred to me as “brilliant and driven” with “a knack for problem-solving like no other,” and appreciated how my innovative ideas kept things running smoothly, along with my natural leadership. Technically, I bring a strong toolkit: Python, R, C++, Java, SQL, Tableau, MongoDB, and cloud tools like AWS. I am also comfortable using the command line, managing version control, and working with backend frameworks like Spring Boot.

I am currently looking to start my career in IT support where I can continue learning. However, I have a long-term goal of bridging tech support and data analysis to make smarter, more informed decisions in any environment.

Feel free to reach out anytime as I am always open to learning, collaborating, or helping solve the next problem!

Projects

Text Classification Using Neural Networks

In this project, I built a neural network model to automatically sort BBC news articles into six topics. I started by cleaning and preparing the textual data by removing common stopwords, tokenizing the text, and ensuring each article was the same length. I then converted the text into sequences that the model could understand. The model used embedding layers to help it understand the relationships between words and dense layers to classify the articles into their topics. After training for 30 epochs, it reached about 95% accuracy on unseen articles. I tracked its progress with performance visualizations. This project enhanced my understanding of natural language processing and gave me valuable experience working with real-world text data and the challenges that come with it.
Tools used: Python, TensorFlow, Keras, NLTK, Matplotlib, NumPy, Excel (CSV file), Google Colab
